Pythag Proof

This is a visual demonstration of the Pythagorean theorem. To convince yourself that the the two small squares add up to the area of the big square, note all the pairs of congruent shapes with the same color.

Note the large square is mostly covered by 4 triangles, all of which are congruent to the right triangle in the middle. Let A be the area of the white/green/yellow triangles. Take 4 times A to cover most of the large square. Then the "hole" is the cyan square. The side of that square is (a-b), where a is the larger leg of the right triangle. So c^2 = 4A + (a-b)^2 = 4(1/2)ab + a^2 - 2ab + b^2 = a^2 + b^2.
